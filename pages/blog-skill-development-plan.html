Create a Plan to Fill the Gap: Creating a Plan to Address the Skills Gap

By Shafira Nurliza Fitri | Published on March 21, 2025

After identifying which skills need improvement or enhancement, the next step is to develop a structured plan for skill development. This is especially important for careers in IT such as UI/UX Design, where tools, user behavior trends, and technology are constantly evolving.

Here's how to create a skills development plan, specifically tailored for a UI/UX career:

1. Choose an Appropriate Learning Method

For the UI/UX field, it's essential to choose learning methods that match your style and schedule:

  • Online Courses:
    Platforms like Coursera, Interaction Design Foundation, UX Design Institute, and Udemy offer beginner to advanced courses in UI/UX. Look for topics like User Research, Wireframing with Figma, or Usability Testing.
  • Workshops & Bootcamps:
    Join intensive bootcamps such as Hacktiv8, RevoU, or Glints Academy, which provide complete UI/UX programs with mentorship and real-life projects.
  • Books & Articles:
    Read books like "Don't Make Me Think" by Steve Krug or "The Design of Everyday Things" by Don Norman. Follow blogs and newsletters from UX Collective, Nielsen Norman Group, or Smashing Magazine.

2. Create a Consistent Study Schedule

  • Set a regular study time, such as one hour each evening or three sessions per week.
  • Set weekly targets, like "Finish 3 Figma modules this week" or "Redesign a login page as a practice."

3. Apply Skills in Real Projects

  • Build a Portfolio:
    Redesign an existing app or create UX case studies based on real-life user experiences. Upload your work to Behance or Notion.
  • Freelance or Volunteer Projects:
    Look for small UI/UX gigs on platforms like Fiverr, Freelancer, or help out a local startup.
  • Collaborate with Developers:
    If possible, work with developers to understand how your designs are implemented into real products.

4. Evaluate Progress Periodically

  • List the skills you want to master: User Flows, Usability Heuristics, Design Systems, Accessibility, etc.
  • Check your progress monthly. Are you faster at creating wireframes? Can you confidently present your designs?
  • If you feel stuck, try a different learning method such as switching from video courses to hands-on projects.

Key Takeaway: With a structured and specific learning plan, you'll not only close your skills gap but also increase your competitiveness as a professional UI/UX designer. Remember, a career in UI/UX isn't just about technical skills, it's about understanding users, communicating clearly, and developing a strong visual eye. All of which can be cultivated with the right plan.
